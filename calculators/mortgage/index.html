📋 What are Closing Costs?

Closing costs are fees and expenses you pay when finalizing your home purchase — in addition to your down payment. They typically range from 2–5% of the loan amount and are due at closing.

  • Loan Origination Fee — Charged by your lender for processing the loan (0.5–1%)
  • Appraisal Fee — Cost to have the home professionally valued ($300–$500)
  • Home Inspection — Professional inspection of the property ($300–$500)
  • Title Insurance — Protects against ownership disputes ($1,000–$2,000)
  • Attorney Fees — Legal review of closing documents ($500–$1,500)
  • Prepaid Items — Upfront property tax and insurance deposits ($1,000–$3,000)

📋 What is PMI?

PMI stands for Private Mortgage Insurance. It's required by lenders when your down payment is less than 20% of the home price.

  • Typically costs 0.5–1% of the loan amount per year
  • Added to your monthly mortgage payment automatically
  • Can be removed once you reach 20% equity in your home
  • To avoid PMI, put down 20% or more
