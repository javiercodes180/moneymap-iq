🚘 New vs Used Car Rates

Used car loans typically come with higher interest rates than new car loans — usually 1–2% more. Here's why:

  • Higher risk for lenders — Used cars depreciate faster and have less value as collateral
  • No manufacturer incentives — New cars often come with promotional 0% or low-rate financing
  • Shorter loan terms recommended — Experts suggest keeping used car loans to 48 months or less to avoid being "underwater" (owing more than the car is worth)

⏱️ The Hidden Cost of Long Loans

A longer loan term means lower monthly payments, but you'll pay significantly more in interest over the life of the loan.

  • 36 months — Highest payments, least interest. Best if you can afford it.
  • 60 months — The sweet spot. Most common term, reasonable payments.
  • 72–84 months — Lowest payments, but you could end up "underwater" — owing more than the car is worth for years.
